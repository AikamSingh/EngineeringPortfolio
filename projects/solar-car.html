Race Critical · University of Michigan Solar Car

Solar Car Steering Wheel

Designing, manufacturing, and deploying the driver interface for a cross-country solar racing vehicle — from first Altium schematic to real-world validation on a 2,000+ mile route.

Timeline Jul 2025 – Present
Role Hardware + Firmware Lead
Tools Altium, STM32Cube IDE, C
Status 2026 deployed · 2027 in design

The Problem

The University of Michigan Solar Car competes in multi-day solar racing events — most notably the World Solar Challenge across the Australian outback. The driver interface has to be race-critical: it must reliably display vehicle telemetry, communicate driver intent to the motor controller over CAN bus, and survive harsh desert conditions with zero tolerance for failure.

The 2025 steering wheel had accumulated technical debt. Push-to-talk noise was a recurring issue, the screen was difficult to read in direct sunlight, and the mechanical assembly was painful because the PCB didn't align well with the enclosure. The team needed a cleaner, more reliable design before Solar Trek 2026.

Design constraint: WSC regulations (section 2.25) require that speed, turn signals, hazard lights, battery warnings, and rear vision images must always be visible to the driver while in motion — not behind menus, not toggle-able.

Requirements

Before touching Altium, I mapped out the hard and soft requirements for the system:

  • CAN bus communicationRequired — all vehicle data
  • DisplayOLED graphical (2026), targeting LCD (2027)
  • Push-to-talkLow-noise hardware implementation
  • Torque / regen paddlesAnalog input to motor controller
  • Panel-mounted hardwareDust ingress reduction
  • Power sourceVehicle energy storage system only (WSC 2.25.2)
  • EnvironmentalSurvive Australian outback conditions
  • Always-visible telemetrySpeed, signals, hazard, battery (WSC 2.25.1)

2027 Redesign

Based on Solar Trek experience, I'm leading the 2027 steering wheel redesign. The main goals:

  • DisplayOLED → 4-line LCD character display
  • Screen placementEvaluating on-wheel vs. column-mounted
  • Column detection sensorIMU or shorted-connection approach
  • Button hardwareTaller panel-mount buttons, new manufacturer
  • PCB integrationAltium into Siemens NX early in cycle
  • Regen paddlesEvaluating repositioning for ergonomics

Safety requirement: The column-detection sensor is race-critical — if the steering wheel is disconnected from the column, torque commands must be disabled to prevent the car from moving uncontrolled. The sensor needs to be reliable and overridable for diagnostics.
